Madame Curie

Marie Curie

The woman who changed the world forever with her groundbreaking research into radioactivity.

Timeline of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Born as 'Maria Salomea Skłodowska' in Warsaw, Poland.
  • 1878 - Her Catholic mother passes away from Tuberculosis. Maria eventually gives up Catholicism.
  • 1883 - Graduated with a Gold Medal from high school.
  • 1884 - Worked as governess to save up for university and taught herself science and mathematics.
  • 1891 - Left Poland for France, and enrolled in the university of Paris.
  • 1893 - Awarded a degree in Physics and began work in an industrial laboratory of Gabriel Lippmann.
  • 1895 - Became married to Pierre Curie, who was also a reliable scientific collaborator.
  • 1898 - Her discovery of radioactive thorium is published, but Gerhard Carl Schmidt had published the same finding two years earlier in Berlin. In the same year, Marie and Pierre also discovered Polonium and Radium.
  • 1900 - Marie becomes the first woman faculty member at the École Normale Supérieure and her husband joined the faculty of the University of Paris.
  • 1902 - 32 papers were published by this time either jointly or separately by the Curies. One announced that, when exposed to radium, diseased, tumour-forming cells were destroyed faster than healthy cells.
  • 1903 - Supervised by Gabriel Lippmann, Marie was awarded her doctorate from the University of Paris. The same year, both Marie and Pierre received the Nobel Prize in Physics.
    • 1906 - Pierre Curie is killed in a road accident. Marie is offered his position and becomes the first woman to become a professor at the University of Paris. She set a goal to establish a world-class lbaoratory as tribute to her husband
  • 1911 - Marie receives her second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry.
  • 1914 - Marie founded the Radium Institute in Paris.
  • 1934 - Marie Curie passed away at the age of 66 from radiation. During World War I, she established x-ray service infrastructure for the military. During the 20's she reached worldwide recognition and toured multiple countries, including the United States, Brazil, Belgium, Spain, and Czechoslovakia.
